Records Management

Records are the complete and unchangeable evidence of what an organization does in its business activities and transactions. Records are an integral part of an organization and hold value as an important information asset. Critical to business continuity, records must be created, organized, secured, maintained and used in a way that effectively supports the activity of an organization as they facilitate and sustain day to day operations. The broad category of records comprises of physical paper files, drawings and blueprints, computer media, X-rays/MRI and CT Scans and all other physical business and personal papers. Each record is important since it facilitates the following:

  • Perform all business transactions on a daily basis
  • Comply with legislative and regulatory requirements
  • Manage business and operations risks
  • Essential for planning, budgeting and business forecasting
  • Provide research documents for development of products and services
  • Preserve the organizational identity and protect the interest of all stakeholders

The Process Chain

  • Initial classification
  • Indexing
  • Pick up
  • Scanning vital documents
  • Storage at our state of art warehouse
  • Retrievals as and when required
  • At the end of the document's lifecycle, environment friendly safe and certified destruction
